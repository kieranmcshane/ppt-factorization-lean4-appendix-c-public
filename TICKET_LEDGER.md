Status key:

- `[x] done`: the ticket's theorem-facing obligation has been supplied,
  eliminated, or ruled out as a viable route.
- `[x] checked-component`: a real subcomponent of an open theorem-strength
  ticket is proved and audited, but the parent ticket remains open.
- `[x] checked-adapter`: the local wrapper/adapter builds and audits cleanly,
  but the underlying mathematical theorem has its own separate open ticket.
- `[x] checked-tooling`: reproducible diagnostics, search, build, or reporting
  infrastructure is checked; it is evidence or workflow, not theorem closure.
- `[x] checked-guardrail`: a Lean-certified no-go or counterexample rules out
  a proposed route or theorem face; it sharpens the target but does not close
  the parent ticket unless the parent objective was exactly that no-go.
- `[x] checked-reduction`: a theorem-facing hypothesis is removed, split, or
  replaced by a strictly smaller natural leaf; the parent ticket remains open
  until the remaining theorem-strength leaves are actually supplied.
- `[ ] active`: the next recommended work item.
- `[ ] open`: genuine theorem-strength work remains.
- `[ ] open-guardrail`: genuine theorem-strength work remains, but the ticket
  is diagnostic rather than the current closure path.
- `[ ] auth-blocked`: operational item that needs user authorization.

Important convention:

- `checked` is not the same as `done`.
- A checked adapter gets its own `[x]` only because that adapter is complete.
  If it still exposes a theorem-strength estimate, that estimate appears below
  as a separate unchecked ticket.
